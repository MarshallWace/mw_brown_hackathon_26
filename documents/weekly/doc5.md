# NFL Week 18 takeaways: What We Learned from Sunday's 14 games

**Published:** Jan 04, 2026 at 04:42 PM  
**Updated:** Jan 05, 2026 at 12:26 AM  
**Author:** Around the NFL Staff  

---

Around The NFL breaks down what you need to know from all of Sunday's action in Week 18 of the 2025 NFL season. Below are the biggest takeaways from each game.

---

## Atlanta Falcons 19, New Orleans Saints 17  
**Kevin Patra**

### Falcons’ victory over Saints sends Panthers to postseason
An odd Week 18 battle for the NFC South ended with Atlanta playing spoiler. A blocked punt and missed Saints field goal helped Atlanta build a lead it protected with defense. Cornerback Dee Alford’s fourth-quarter interception sealed the win.

### Atlanta defense propels Raheem Morris’ team
The Falcons’ offense sputtered, generating just 208 yards, but the defense dominated. Rookie edge rusher James Pearce Jr. recorded back-to-back sacks and helped Atlanta set a franchise record with 57 sacks. *(Editor’s update: Atlanta fired Morris and GM Terry Fontenot after the game.)*

### Tyler Shough flashes despite Saints loss
Shorthanded New Orleans leaned on rookie QB Tyler Shough, who threw for 259 yards and a touchdown and added a rushing score. Despite a costly interception, Shough continued to show poise and promise under pressure.

---

## Cleveland Browns 20, Cincinnati Bengals 18  
**Nick Shook**

### Myles Garrett stamps his place in history
Garrett broke the NFL single-season sack record with his 23rd sack, surpassing Michael Strahan and T.J. Watt. Despite constant extra attention, Garrett’s impact reshaped opposing game plans all season.

### Bengals fall in familiar fashion
Cincinnati outgained Cleveland and controlled possession, but defensive breakdowns doomed them again. A late Browns drive ended with Andre Szmyt’s 49-yard game-winning field goal.

### Browns defense finishes strong
Despite injuries across the secondary, Cleveland’s defense produced two defensive touchdowns and controlled the game for long stretches, underscoring how vital the unit was to the Browns’ season.

---

## Houston Texans 38, Indianapolis Colts 30  
**Bobby Kownack**

### Texans pack it in at halftime
With division hopes fading, Houston rested starters after halftime. C.J. Stroud impressed early, throwing and running for touchdowns, while Danielle Hunter added a strip-sack to reach 15 on the season.

### Riley Leonard looks like he belongs
In his first career start, Leonard threw two touchdowns, ran for another, and showed arm talent and mobility. Mistakes followed, but the debut was encouraging against the NFL’s top defense.

### Alec Pierce shines — then exits
Pierce caught four passes for 132 yards and two touchdowns, surpassing 1,000 yards on the season, before an emotional ejection cut his day short.

---

## Jacksonville Jaguars 41, Tennessee Titans 7  
**Jeremy Bergman**

### Jaguars jaunt into postseason
Jacksonville scored 41 unanswered points after an early Titans touchdown. Trevor Lawrence threw three TDs, Cam Little drilled a 67-yard field goal, and the Jaguars secured the AFC South title and a 13-win season.

### Cam Ward’s rookie season ends abruptly
Ward scored on a QB run before injuring his shoulder and exiting. Despite inconsistency, the Titans saw enough promise to believe in his long-term upside.

### Lawrence caps best season with franchise records
Lawrence finished with 38 total touchdowns and surpassed 4,000 passing yards, leading Jacksonville into the playoffs on an eight-game win streak.

---

## Minnesota Vikings 16, Green Bay Packers 3  
**Eric Edholm**

### J.J. McCarthy exits early again
McCarthy returned from injury but left in the third quarter after re-injuring his throwing hand, reigniting concerns about durability.

### Packers rest starters, gain depth reps
Green Bay rested key players and started Clayton Tune. While the offense struggled, reserve defenders gained valuable experience.

### Emotional sendoffs in Minnesota
Possible departures for DC Brian Flores, Harrison Smith, and C.J. Ham made for a bittersweet but memorable home finale.

---

## New York Giants 34, Dallas Cowboys 17  
**Michael Baca**

### Jaxson Dart caps rookie season in style
Dart threw two touchdowns, avoided turnovers, and ended the Giants’ long losing streak to Dallas.

### Cowboys sit Dak, evaluate youth
Dallas rested Prescott, giving reps to Joe Milton III and Jaydon Blue, who impressed with 64 rushing yards and a touchdown.

### Mike Kafka shines as interim coach
The Giants scored on seven of 10 possessions, closing the season with back-to-back wins under Kafka’s offensive leadership.

---

## Buffalo Bills 35, New York Jets 8  
**Eric Edholm**

### Bills rest stars, dominate anyway
Buffalo limited Josh Allen’s exposure and still routed the Jets. Ray Davis posted career highs with 151 rushing yards.

### Jets finish season without interception
New York became the first modern team to play an entire season without a single interception, underscoring massive defensive deficiencies.

### Matt Prater injury bears watching
Buffalo lost its kicker to a quad injury, an issue that could loom large in the postseason.

---

## Detroit Lions 19, Chicago Bears 16  
**Kevin Patra**

### Bears rally late, still earn No. 2 seed
Chicago erased a fourth-quarter deficit but lost on a last-second field goal. Despite the loss, the Bears secured the NFC’s No. 2 seed.

### Lions avoid losing season
Detroit finished 9-8, with Jared Goff throwing for 331 yards and Amon-Ra St. Brown dominating coverage.

### Caleb Williams shows late-game poise
After a slow start, Williams nearly led another comeback, finishing the season with a Bears franchise-record 3,942 passing yards.

---

## Denver Broncos 19, Los Angeles Chargers 3  
**Kevin Patra**

### Broncos defense clinches No. 1 seed
Denver forced turnovers and coasted past a Chargers team resting starters to secure the AFC’s top seed.

### Denver offense remains a concern
Bo Nix struggled to push the ball downfield, finishing with just 141 passing yards in a conservative outing.

### Trey Lance overwhelmed
Lance threw a pick-six and struggled against pressure, reinforcing questions about his future as more than a backup.

---

## Los Angeles Rams 37, Arizona Cardinals 20  
**Nick Shook**

### Stafford rebounds at right time
Matthew Stafford threw three touchdowns and looked sharp after a sluggish first half, reviving his MVP momentum.

### Cardinals play aggressively
Arizona used trick plays and downfield shots to stay competitive, earning praise for effort despite the loss.

### Rams tune up for playoffs
Los Angeles rediscovered offensive rhythm and secured the No. 5 seed heading into January.

---

## Las Vegas Raiders 14, Kansas City Chiefs 12  
**Michael Baca**

### Raiders snap skid behind Carlson’s leg
Daniel Carlson drilled a 60-yard game-winner as Las Vegas ended a 10-game losing streak.

### Kelce adds to legendary résumé
Possibly his final game, Travis Kelce reached 13,000 career receiving yards, cementing Hall of Fame status.

### Raiders defense dominates sans Crosby
Eight sacks and relentless pressure powered Las Vegas’ upset despite missing Maxx Crosby.

---

## New England Patriots 38, Miami Dolphins 10  
**Eric Edholm**

### Patriots clinch No. 2 seed convincingly
Drake Maye and the run game overwhelmed Miami, while returning linemen bolstered both sides of the trenches.

### Dolphins’ season ends with frustration
Quinn Ewers flashed but turnovers and penalties plagued Miami in a disappointing finale.

### Returning players provide boost
Milton Williams and rookie tackle Will Campbell made immediate impacts ahead of the postseason.

---

## Washington Commanders 24, Philadelphia Eagles 17  
**Nick Shook**

### Josh Johnson delivers storybook win
At 39, Johnson accounted for two fourth-quarter touchdowns, leading Washington past Eagles backups.

### Tanner McKee struggles
McKee’s tape raised questions about his readiness after accuracy and pressure issues.

### Commanders finish with pride
Despite a disappointing season, Washington closed strong with effort and resilience.

---

## Pittsburgh Steelers 26, Baltimore Ravens 24  
**Grant Gordon**

### Rodgers, Steelers claim AFC North
Aaron Rodgers led three comebacks, capping the night with a game-winning TD to Calvin Austin III.

### Ravens fall short again
Despite big plays from Lamar Jackson, Baltimore’s season ended on a missed field goal.

### Uncertain future in Baltimore
Another late collapse may prompt major changes for the Ravens after unmet expectations.
