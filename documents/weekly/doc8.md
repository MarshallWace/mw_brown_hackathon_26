# NFL Week 15 takeaways: What We Learned from Sunday's 14 games

*Published: Dec 14, 2025 at 04:12 PM*  
*Updated: Dec 14, 2025 at 11:48 PM*  
*By Around the NFL Staff*

Around The NFL breaks down what you need to know from all of Sunday's action in Week 15 of the 2025 NFL season.

---

## EARLY GAMES

### Chicago Bears 31, Cleveland Browns 3

**Nick Shook's takeaways:**

**Takeaway masters do it again.**  
Chicago’s defense continued its league-leading opportunism, intercepting Shedeur Sanders three times and converting each turnover into points. The Bears’ energy and execution fueled their most complete defensive performance of the season.

**This feels like the end of the Kevin Stefanski era.**  
Cleveland was thoroughly outclassed in all phases, never threatening Chicago and showing little fight in brutal conditions. With few signs of progress, major changes appear inevitable.

**Caleb Williams is a magician.**  
Williams shined in freezing conditions, extending plays and delivering jaw-dropping throws, including a spectacular off-platform TD to DJ Moore. His growth mirrors Chicago’s ascent.

---

### Baltimore Ravens 24, Cincinnati Bengals 0

**Eric Edholm's takeaways:**

**Ravens defense bottled up Burrow.**  
Baltimore shut out Joe Burrow for the first time in his career, forcing turnovers and dominating up front to keep playoff hopes alive.

**Bengals defense did its job, but offense failed.**  
Cincinnati wasted a strong defensive showing with offensive inefficiency and costly mistakes, effectively ending slim postseason hopes.

**Ravens leaned on the run.**  
Derrick Henry, Lamar Jackson and a punishing ground game paired with dominant defense delivered a must-win victory.

---

### Houston Texans 40, Arizona Cardinals 20

**Eric Edholm's takeaways:**

**Texans offense shows needed growth.**  
C.J. Stroud led an efficient offense that scored on its first five drives, giving Houston momentum heading toward the postseason.

**Trey McBride shines in lost season.**  
McBride reached 100 catches and 1,000 yards again, remaining Arizona’s most consistent and dangerous weapon.

**Defense still stout, but cracks appeared.**  
Houston struggled containing McBride, an area to monitor with several strong tight ends ahead.

---

### Jacksonville Jaguars 48, New York Jets 20

**Jeremy Bergman's takeaways:**

**Trevor Lawrence obliterates New York.**  
Lawrence delivered a historic performance with five passing TDs, one rushing TD and over 50 rushing yards, powering Jacksonville’s fifth straight win.

**Jets defense D.O.A. again.**  
New York fell behind early and never recovered, continuing a disastrous defensive season amid coaching changes.

**Jaguars stay atop AFC South.**  
At 10-4, Jacksonville controls its destiny and remains in the hunt for a first-round bye.

---

### Los Angeles Chargers 16, Kansas City Chiefs 13

**Nick Shook's takeaways:**

**Chargers win another rock fight.**  
Los Angeles leaned on toughness and timely interceptions to complete a season sweep of Kansas City.

**Chiefs eliminated with a thud.**  
Late turnovers and a Patrick Mahomes knee injury sealed Kansas City’s first playoff miss since 2014.

**Herbert defies adversity.**  
Despite constant pressure and a fractured hand, Justin Herbert continued to carry the Chargers.

---

### Buffalo Bills 35, New England Patriots 31

**Kevin Patra's takeaways:**

**Allen flips the script.**  
Josh Allen ignited a second-half explosion after a slow start, leading five straight TD drives.

**Patriots collapse late.**  
New England dominated early but faltered defensively and offensively as Buffalo surged.

**AFC East race tightens.**  
Despite the loss, the Patriots still control their division destiny.

---

### Washington Commanders 29, New York Giants 21

**Bobby Kownack's takeaways:**

**Defense and special teams swing momentum.**  
A Jaylin Lane punt-return TD and late interception before halftime proved decisive.

**Abdul Carter responds.**  
After discipline issues earlier this season, Carter delivered his best performance as a pro.

**Offensive sparks for Washington.**  
Jacory Croskey-Merritt and Terry McLaurin provided rare bright spots in a difficult season.

---

### Philadelphia Eagles 31, Las Vegas Raiders 0

**Kevin Patra's takeaways:**

**Eagles offense rebounds.**  
Jalen Hurts dissected the Raiders as Philly snapped a three-game skid in emphatic fashion.

**Defense dominates again.**  
Vic Fangio’s unit smothered Las Vegas, pitching a shutout and continuing a dominant stretch.

**Pickett struggles vs former team.**  
Las Vegas’ offense was completely overwhelmed, generating just 75 total yards.

---

## LATE WINDOW

### Denver Broncos 34, Green Bay Packers 26

**Eric Edholm's takeaways:**

**Surtain’s interception changed everything.**  
Patrick Surtain II’s pick flipped momentum as Denver closed with authority.

**Packers battered by injuries.**  
Green Bay lost multiple key contributors, including Micah Parsons to a feared ACL tear.

**Bo Nix shines.**  
Nix threw four TDs and avoided sacks, continuing Denver’s surge toward contention.

---

### Los Angeles Rams 41, Detroit Lions 34

**Kevin Patra's takeaways:**

**Rams dominate second half.**  
Matthew Stafford and Puka Nacua overwhelmed Detroit after halftime to secure a statement win.

**Lions fade late.**  
Detroit’s explosive offense stalled after halftime, putting playoff hopes in jeopardy.

**Rams clinch playoff berth.**  
At 11-3, Los Angeles locked in a postseason spot and retained the NFC’s top seed.

---

### New Orleans Saints 20, Carolina Panthers 17

**Nick Shook's takeaways:**

**Tyler Shough shows ice-cold poise.**  
The rookie QB engineered another late comeback, showcasing confidence and growth.

**Panthers squander opportunity.**  
Carolina failed to close, committing penalties and miscues in crunch time.

**Kellen Moore’s impact shows.**  
New Orleans is playing with renewed energy despite a rebuilding roster.

---

### Seattle Seahawks 18, Indianapolis Colts 16

**Bobby Kownack's takeaways:**

**Seahawks survive a slog.**  
Seattle relied on Jason Myers’ six field goals and late execution to escape.

**Rivers’ return falls short.**  
Philip Rivers showed grit but lacked the arm strength to finish the comeback.

**Defense carries Seattle.**  
The Seahawks’ championship-caliber defense limited Jonathan Taylor and saved the day.

---

### San Francisco 49ers 37, Tennessee Titans 24

**Michael Baca's takeaways:**

**Purdy powers efficient rout.**  
Brock Purdy delivered a clean, dominant performance with three passing TDs.

**Simmons provides highlight moment.**  
Jeffery Simmons’ strip-sack and TD catch were bright spots in Tennessee’s loss.

**Defense active, but imperfect.**  
San Francisco flew around but missed turnover opportunities.

---

## SUNDAY NIGHT

### Minnesota Vikings 34, Dallas Cowboys 26

**Grant Gordon's takeaways:**

**Vikings defense crushes Cowboys’ hopes.**  
Brian Flores’ blitz-heavy scheme overwhelmed Dallas and nearly ended their playoff chances.

**Prescott, offense sputter.**  
Despite solid numbers, the Cowboys couldn’t convert in key moments.

**McCarthy grows, Jefferson chemistry lacking.**  
J.J. McCarthy rebounded from an early mistake, but connection with Justin Jefferson remains a concern.
